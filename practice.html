Exploring the Cultural Heart of Dhaka: University of Dhaka

Discovering Dhaka's Academic Jewel

Imagine stepping into a world where history, culture, and academic pursuit converge seamlessly. That’s exactly what you experience when you visit the University of Dhaka. Established in 1921, this institution is more than just a university—it's a symbol of Bangladesh's rich heritage and its relentless pursuit of knowledge.

A Walk Through Time

As you stroll through the campus, the first thing that catches your eye is the striking Curzon Hall. This architectural gem, with its distinctive red-brick exterior and imposing dome, feels like a portal to the past. Built during the British colonial era, Curzon Hall now houses the Faculty of Sciences. Its serene courtyard and lush gardens provide a peaceful respite from Dhaka's bustling streets.

Further into the campus, you’ll find the elegant white edifice of the Arts Building, home to the Faculty of Arts. Its classical style, complete with grand columns, stands as a testament to the university’s longstanding commitment to the humanities. Walking along the shaded pathways, it’s easy to lose yourself in thought, imagining the generations of students who have roamed these grounds before you.
